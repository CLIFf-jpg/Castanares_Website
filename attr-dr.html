Drottningholm Palace

The beloved home of the Swedish Royal Family and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1991.

About Drottningholm Palace

Drottningholm Palace is the private residence of the Swedish Royal Family and one of Sweden's best-preserved royal castles. It is located on Lovön island in Lake Mälaren, about 11 kilometers west of Stockholm.

Built in the late 17th century, the palace is often compared to the Palace of Versailles in France due to its grand baroque architecture, extensive formal gardens, and magnificent interiors. The palace was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1991.

Parts of the palace are open to the public year-round. The grounds feature a stunning baroque garden, an 18th-century Chinese Pavilion, and a beautifully preserved court theatre that still hosts opera performances today.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Drottningholm, Ekerö Municipality
  • Getting There: Boat from Stockholm City Hall (most scenic route)
  • Open: Palace open year-round (some areas seasonal)
  • Highlights: State Apartments, Theatre, Chinese Pavilion, Gardens
  • Best for: History lovers, architecture fans, nature walks
  • Time Needed: Half day to full day
