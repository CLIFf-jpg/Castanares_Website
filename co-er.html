About Ericsson

Telefonaktiebolaget LM Ericsson, commonly known as Ericsson, is a Swedish multinational networking and telecommunications company headquartered in Stockholm. It is one of the leading providers of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) to service providers in the world.

Ericsson was founded in 1876 by Lars Magnus Ericsson, who initially repaired telegraph equipment and went on to manufacture telephones. The company played a major role in the development of mobile communications, including 2G, 3G, 4G, and now 5G networks.

Today, Ericsson's equipment is used by over 1,000 networks in more than 180 countries. The company's infrastructure powers the smartphones and devices of billions of people around the globe.

Key Facts

  • Founded: 1876 in Stockholm, Sweden
  • Founder: Lars Magnus Ericsson
  • Headquarters: Stockholm, Sweden
  • Employees: Approximately 100,000 worldwide
  • Networks Served: 1,000+ in 180+ countries
  • Key Focus: 5G technology, cloud infrastructure
  • Listed on: NASDAQ (ERIC)
