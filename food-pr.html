Princess Cake (Prinsesstårta)

A Swedish classic of layers, cream, and beautiful green marzipan.

What Is a Princess Cake?

The Prinsesstårta (Princess Cake) is one of Sweden's most iconic and beloved cakes. It is a layered sponge cake filled with raspberry jam, pastry cream, and a thick dome of whipped cream, all covered in a smooth layer of bright green marzipan.

It is traditionally decorated with a pink marzipan rose on top, and the edge is often dusted with powdered sugar. The combination of soft sponge, sweet jam, creamy filling, and the slightly almond-flavored marzipan makes it an unforgettable dessert.

The cake was created in the 1920s by Jenny Åkerström, a home economics teacher who taught the Swedish princesses — hence the name.

Key Layers and Ingredients

  • Sponge cake (genoise) — 3 layers
  • Raspberry or strawberry jam
  • Vanilla pastry cream
  • Freshly whipped cream (generous dome)
  • Green marzipan (for covering)
  • Pink marzipan rose (for decoration)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)
