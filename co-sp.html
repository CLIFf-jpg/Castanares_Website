From Stockholm to the world — the platform that revolutionized the music industry.

About Spotify

Spotify is a Swedish audio streaming and media services provider, founded in 2006 by Daniel Ek and Martin Lorentzon in Stockholm, Sweden. It officially launched in 2008 and has since grown into the world's largest music streaming platform.

Spotify offers access to millions of songs, podcasts, and audiobooks from creators all over the world. The service is available in a free, ad-supported version or as a premium subscription without ads.

Spotify has been credited with helping combat music piracy by providing a legal, easy, and affordable alternative. The company went public on the New York Stock Exchange in 2018 and has offices in over 80 countries.

Key Facts

  • Founded: 2006 in Stockholm, Sweden
  • Founders: Daniel Ek and Martin Lorentzon
  • Headquarters: Stockholm, Sweden
  • Monthly Active Users: Over 600 million
  • Songs Available: Over 100 million tracks
  • Markets: Available in 180+ countries
  • Listed on: New York Stock Exchange (NYSE: SPOT)
