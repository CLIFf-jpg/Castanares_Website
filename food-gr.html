Gravlax

Cured salmon — a refined Swedish delicacy with centuries of tradition.

Gravlax

Sweden's famous cured salmon dish, served as a starter or on open-faced sandwiches.

What Is Gravlax?

Gravlax (also spelled gravlaks) is a Nordic dish consisting of raw salmon that has been cured with salt, sugar, and dill. The word "grav" means "grave" in Swedish, referring to the old tradition of burying the cured fish in the ground to ferment it.

Today, gravlax is made without burying — instead, the salmon is simply refrigerated for 24 to 48 hours under the cure mixture. The result is a silky, slightly sweet, and very flavorful salmon that is sliced thin and served cold.

It is commonly served on crispbread or rye bread, accompanied by a mustard-dill sauce called hovmästarsås.

Key Ingredients

  • Fresh salmon fillet (skin-on)
  • Coarse salt
  • White sugar
  • Fresh dill (a generous amount)
  • White pepper or pink peppercorns
  • Aquavit (optional, for flavor)
  • Mustard and dill sauce for serving
