Swedish Meatballs

Köttbullar — a timeless Swedish classic loved around the world.

Swedish Meatballs (Köttbullar)

One of Sweden's most iconic dishes, enjoyed at home and around the world.

What Are Swedish Meatballs?

Swedish Meatballs, known in Sweden as Köttbullar, are small, round meatballs made from a mix of ground beef and pork. They are seasoned with allspice and nutmeg, giving them a distinct warm, slightly sweet flavor.

They are traditionally served with creamy brown gravy, lingonberry jam, boiled or mashed potatoes, and pickled cucumber. This combination of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors is what makes the dish so beloved.

The dish became internationally famous largely because of IKEA, which serves Köttbullar in its restaurant. Today, millions of meatballs are served every day in IKEA restaurants across the world.

Key Ingredients

  • Ground beef and pork (mixed)
  • Breadcrumbs soaked in cream
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Egg
  • Allspice and nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper
  • Butter (for frying)
  • Cream and beef stock (for gravy)
