Kanelbulle (Cinnamon Bun)

Soft, fragrant, and utterly irresistible — Sweden's most beloved pastry.

What Is a Kanelbulle?

A Kanelbulle is a Swedish cinnamon bun. Unlike the American version, Swedish cinnamon buns are flavored with cardamom in the dough itself, which gives them a distinctive warm, floral flavor. They are then filled with butter, cinnamon, and sugar, rolled up, and topped with pearl sugar before baking.

In Sweden, the Kanelbulle is deeply connected to Fika — the Swedish tradition of taking a break with coffee and a sweet treat. Fika is so important in Swedish culture that it is practically a social institution.

Sweden even has an official Cinnamon Bun Day (Kanelbullens dag) on October 4th every year, celebrated since 1999.

Key Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Milk (warm)
  • Butter
  • Sugar
  • Yeast (dry or fresh)
  • Ground cardamom (in the dough)
  • Cinnamon (for the filling)
  • Pearl sugar (for topping)
  • Egg (for brushing)
