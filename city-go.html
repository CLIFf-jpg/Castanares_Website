Sweden's second-largest city — a lively port city on the west coast known for its culture and cuisine.

About Gothenburg

Gothenburg (Swedish: Göteborg) is the second-largest city in Sweden, located on the country's west coast along the Göta älv river. With a population of around 600,000 in the city and 1 million in the greater area, it is a major industrial, commercial, and cultural center.

Gothenburg was founded in 1621 by King Gustav II Adolf and has a strong history as a port and trading city. Today it is home to Volvo's headquarters and one of Europe's largest seaports.

The city is known for its friendly, relaxed atmosphere compared to Stockholm. Its Avenyn boulevard is lined with cafés and restaurants, while the Haga district is famous for its charming wooden houses and cozy cafés.

Key Facts

  • Founded: 1621 by King Gustav II Adolf
  • Population: ~600,000 in city, ~1 million metro
  • Location: West coast, on the Göta älv river
  • Known for: Volvo, seafood, Liseberg amusement park
  • University: University of Gothenburg (founded 1891)
  • Airport: Gothenburg Landvetter Airport
  • Annual Festival: Way Out West music festival
