Malmö

Sweden's third-largest city — bold architecture, diversity, and a bridge to Europe.

About Malmö

Malmö is the third-largest city in Sweden, located in the province of Scania (Skåne) at the very southern tip of the country. With a population of around 350,000, it is one of Scandinavia's most multicultural cities — over 170 nationalities are represented among its residents.

Malmö is famous for the Öresund Bridge, a combined rail and road bridge-tunnel that connects the city to Copenhagen, Denmark. Opened in 2000, the bridge has fundamentally transformed the region, creating the Øresund Region — one of the most dynamic cross-border metropolitan areas in Europe.

The city is known for its bold, innovative architecture. The Turning Torso — a twisting residential skyscraper designed by Santiago Calatrava — is the tallest building in Scandinavia and one of the most iconic pieces of modern architecture in Europe.

Key Facts

  • Population: ~350,000
  • Location: Southernmost city in Sweden (Scania)
  • Famous for: Öresund Bridge, Turning Torso
  • Connected to: Copenhagen, Denmark (17 min by train)
  • University: Malmö University (founded 1998)
  • Airport: Malmö Airport / Copenhagen Airport (nearby)
  • Nationalities: 170+ represented in the city
