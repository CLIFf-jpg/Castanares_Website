A global fashion giant — affordable style from Sweden to every corner of the world.

About H&M

H&M (Hennes & Mauritz) is a Swedish multinational clothing retail company known for its fast-fashion clothing for men, women, teenagers, and children. It was founded in 1947 by Erling Persson in Västerås, Sweden, originally under the name "Hennes" (meaning "Hers" in Swedish).

The company acquired a hunting and fishing equipment store called Mauritz Widforss in 1968, adding menswear to its lineup and becoming "Hennes & Mauritz." The brand is now headquartered in Stockholm and operates over 4,700 stores in 76 markets worldwide.

H&M is the second largest global clothing retailer in the world and has made an effort in recent years to incorporate more sustainable and recycled materials into its clothing lines.

Key Facts

  • Founded: 1947 in Västerås, Sweden
  • Founder: Erling Persson
  • Headquarters: Stockholm, Sweden
  • Stores: Over 4,700 in 76 countries
  • Products: Clothing, accessories, homeware
  • Position: 2nd largest fashion retailer in the world
  • Sustainability: H&M Conscious Collection uses recycled materials
