Volvo

A Swedish automotive legend — the brand that put safety first.

About Volvo

Volvo is a Swedish automotive manufacturer founded in 1927 in Gothenburg, Sweden. The company's name comes from the Latin word "volvere," meaning "to roll." Volvo is one of the world's most recognized car brands, renowned above all for its commitment to safety.

In 1959, Volvo engineer Nils Bohlin invented the three-point safety belt — now standard in every car in the world. Rather than patenting it exclusively, Volvo made the design freely available to all car manufacturers, a decision that has saved an estimated one million lives.

Today, Volvo Cars is owned by the Chinese company Geely and is headquartered in Gothenburg. The company has committed to becoming fully electric by 2030.

Key Facts

  • Founded: 1927 in Gothenburg, Sweden
  • Founders: Assar Gabrielsson and Gustaf Larson
  • Headquarters: Gothenburg, Sweden
  • Owner: Geely (Chinese multinational)
  • Known for: Safety innovations, Scandinavian design
  • Key Invention: Three-point seatbelt (1959)
  • Goal: Fully electric by 2030
