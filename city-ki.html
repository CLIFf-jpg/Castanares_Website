Kiruna

Sweden's northernmost city, located above the Arctic Circle in the land of midnight sun and Northern Lights.

About Kiruna

Kiruna is the northernmost city in Sweden, located approximately 145 km above the Arctic Circle in Swedish Lapland. With a population of about 23,000 people, it is a small city, but one of the most unique and fascinating in the world.

Kiruna was founded in 1900 to support the massive iron ore mine that exists beneath and around the city. The Kiruna mine, operated by LKAB (a Swedish state mining company), is the world's largest and most modern underground iron ore mine. The ore has been mined here for over a century.

In a remarkable engineering and urban planning feat, the entire city of Kiruna is currently being moved three kilometers to the east — because the mining has caused the ground beneath the original city to subside. The city relocation is expected to be completed by 2035.

Key Facts

  • Population: ~23,000
  • Location: 145 km above the Arctic Circle
  • Founded: 1900 (due to iron ore mining)
  • Famous for: Iron ore mine, Northern Lights, Icehotel nearby
  • Midnight Sun: From late May to mid-July
  • Polar Night: November to January
  • Airport: Kiruna Airport (KRN)
