Icehotel

The world's first hotel made of ice, rebuilt each winter in Jukkasjärvi, Swedish Lapland.

About the Icehotel

The Icehotel in Jukkasjärvi, Sweden is the world's first hotel constructed entirely from ice and snow. Located about 200 km above the Arctic Circle, the hotel is rebuilt every year using ice harvested from the nearby Torne River.

The hotel was first created in 1989 and has since become one of the world's most extraordinary and sought-after travel experiences. Each winter, artists from around the world are invited to create unique rooms and suites — making every room a work of art. No two rooms are ever the same.

In addition to the seasonal ice hotel, there is now a permanent year-round version called Icehotel 365, which is kept frozen using solar energy, allowing guests to experience the icy wonder even in summer.

Key Facts

  • Location: Jukkasjärvi, Swedish Lapland
  • First Built: 1989
  • Temperature Inside: -5°C to -8°C (guests sleep in thermal sleeping bags)
  • Ice Used: 2,000+ tonnes of ice per year
  • Nearest Airport: Kiruna Airport (15 minutes away)
  • Icehotel 365: Open year-round (permanent structure)
  • Activities: Northern Lights tours, dog sledding, snowmobiling
