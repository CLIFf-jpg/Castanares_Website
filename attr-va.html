One of the world's best-preserved 17th-century warships — a must-see in Stockholm.

Vasa Museum

Home to the only surviving 17th-century warship in the world — the Vasa.

About the Vasa Museum

The Vasa Museum in Stockholm is the most visited museum in Scandinavia, attracting over 1.5 million visitors every year. The centerpiece is the Vasa warship, a massive Swedish Navy vessel that sank on its maiden voyage in 1628 — just 1,300 meters from shore.

The ship was salvaged from the bottom of Stockholm harbor in 1961 after lying underwater for 333 years. Because the cold, low-salinity Baltic water preserved the wood remarkably well, about 98% of the original ship is intact today.

The museum was built entirely around the ship itself. Visitors can walk around the ship on multiple levels and see thousands of original artifacts, sculptures, and cannons recovered from the wreck.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Galärvarvsvägen 14, Stockholm
  • Open: Daily (check website for hours)
  • Entry Fee: Adult ticket required
  • Best for: History lovers, families, school groups
  • Getting There: Ferry from Slussen or Nybroplan
  • Time Needed: 2 to 3 hours
  • Language: Exhibits in Swedish and English
