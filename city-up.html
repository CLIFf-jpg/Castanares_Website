Uppsala

Sweden's fourth-largest city — a center of learning, history, and Viking heritage.

About Uppsala

Uppsala is Sweden's fourth-largest city, located about 70 kilometers north of Stockholm. It has a population of approximately 230,000 people and is one of the most important cities in Swedish history and culture.

Uppsala is home to Uppsala University — the oldest university in the Nordic countries, founded in 1477. With over 50,000 students, the university gives the city a lively, youthful energy and makes it an important center for research, culture, and innovation.

Uppsala Cathedral (Uppsala domkyrka) is the largest church in the Nordic countries and one of the most impressive Gothic cathedrals in Northern Europe. It is the burial site of King Gustav Vasa and the botanist Carl Linnaeus, who lived and worked in Uppsala.

Key Facts

  • Population: ~350,000
  • Location: Southernmost city in Sweden (Scania)
  • Famous for: Öresund Bridge, Turning Torso
  • Connected to: Copenhagen, Denmark (17 min by train)
  • University: Malmö University (founded 1998)
  • Airport: Malmö Airport / Copenhagen Airport (nearby)
  • Nationalities: 170+ represented in the city
