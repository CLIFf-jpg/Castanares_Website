Gamla Stan (Old Town)

The heart of Stockholm — a medieval island of winding alleys and historic buildings.

About Gamla Stan

Gamla Stan (meaning "Old Town") is the historic heart of Stockholm, built on a small island called Stadsholmen. It is one of the best-preserved medieval city centers in Europe, with streets and buildings dating back to the 13th century.

Walking through Gamla Stan feels like stepping back in time. The narrow cobblestone alleys, colorful ochre and red buildings, and centuries-old churches create a magical atmosphere that attracts millions of visitors every year.

Key landmarks include the Royal Palace (Kungliga Slottet), Stockholm Cathedral (Storkyrkan), and the Nobel Museum. The main street, Västerlånggatan, is lined with souvenir shops, cafés, and restaurants.

Top Things to Do in Gamla Stan

  • Visit the Royal Palace and watch the Changing of the Guard
  • Walk through Mårten Trotzigs Gränd — the narrowest alley in Stockholm
  • Visit the Nobel Museum on Stortorget Square
  • See Storkyrkan (Stockholm Cathedral)
  • Explore antique shops and local boutiques
  • Try traditional Swedish food at a local restaurant
  • Visit the Swedish Parliament building (Riksdagshuset)
