Smörgåsbord

The great Swedish buffet tradition — a celebration of food, family, and community.

What Is a Smörgåsbord?

A Smörgåsbord (literally "sandwich table") is a traditional Swedish buffet-style meal featuring a wide variety of cold and hot dishes. It is one of Sweden's most important culinary traditions and is especially popular during Christmas (Julbord) and Midsommar celebrations.

The meal follows a specific order: guests start with herring dishes, then move to cold fish (like gravlax), followed by cold meats and salads, then hot dishes like meatballs and Janssons frestelse, and finally desserts.

The word "smörgåsbord" has entered the English language as a term for any large, varied selection of things.

Typical Dishes on a Smörgåsbord

  • Pickled herring (sill) — multiple varieties
  • Gravlax with mustard-dill sauce
  • Swedish meatballs (Köttbullar)
  • Janssons frestelse (anchovy and potato gratin)
  • Deviled eggs
  • Rye bread and crispbread
  • Prinskorv (small sausages)
  • Red beet salad
  • Rice pudding (Risgrynsgröt)
