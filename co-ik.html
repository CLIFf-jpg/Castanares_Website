IKEA

The world's largest furniture retailer — born in the Swedish countryside.

IKEA

From a small mail-order business in Sweden to the world's most recognized furniture brand.

About IKEA

IKEA is a Swedish multinational conglomerate that designs and sells ready-to-assemble furniture, kitchen appliances, and home accessories. It was founded in 1943 by Ingvar Kamprad in Älmhult, a small town in Småland, Sweden, when he was just 17 years old.

The name IKEA is an acronym of the founder's initials (I.K.) plus the first letters of Elmtaryd (the farm where he grew up) and Agunnaryd (his home village). Today, IKEA is the world's largest furniture retailer, with over 460 stores in more than 60 countries.

IKEA is also famous for its unique in-store experience — its large blue-and-yellow warehouse stores are designed as a one-way journey through room displays, ending at a warehouse where customers pick up flat-pack boxes to assemble at home.

Key Facts

  • Founded: 1943 in Älmhult, Sweden
  • Founder: Ingvar Kamprad
  • Headquarters: Leiden, Netherlands (holding company)
  • Stores worldwide: 460+ in 60+ countries
  • Annual visitors: Over 775 million per year
  • Swedish Meatballs served: 150 million+ per year
  • IKEA Colors: Blue and Yellow (Swedish flag colors)
