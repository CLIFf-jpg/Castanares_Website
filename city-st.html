Stockholm

The capital and largest city of Sweden — a beautiful city spread across 14 islands.

About Stockholm

Stockholm is the capital and most populous city of Sweden, with a metropolitan population of over 2.3 million people. Founded in the 13th century, the city is built on 14 islands at the mouth of Lake Mälaren, where the lake meets the Baltic Sea.

Stockholm is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful capital cities in Europe. Its waterways, parks, and historic architecture make it a joy to explore on foot, by bike, or by boat. The city is also a global hub for technology, design, fashion, and music.

Stockholm is home to many world-class museums, including the Vasa Museum, the Abba Museum, the National Museum of Sweden, and the Nobel Prize Museum. The city hosted the 1912 Summer Olympics.

Key Facts

  • Founded: Around 1252
  • Population: 2.3 million (metro area)
  • Built on: 14 islands
  • Currency: Swedish Krona (SEK)
  • Airport: Arlanda International Airport
  • Famous for: ABBA, Nobel Prize, Vasa Museum, Gamla Stan
  • Nickname: "Venice of the North"
