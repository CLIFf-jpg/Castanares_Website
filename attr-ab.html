Abisko National Park

Arctic wilderness, Northern Lights, and Sweden's most breathtaking natural landscapes.

About Abisko National Park

Abisko National Park is located in the far north of Sweden, in Swedish Lapland, about 200 kilometers above the Arctic Circle. It is one of Sweden's most popular national parks and is world-famous for its exceptional conditions for viewing the Aurora Borealis (Northern Lights).

The park was established in 1909, making it one of the oldest national parks in Europe. It covers an area of about 77 km² and sits along the southern shore of Lake Torneträsk, surrounded by dramatic mountains, ancient forests, and vast Arctic tundra.

Thanks to a unique microclimate created by the surrounding mountains, Abisko has unusually clear skies and very low precipitation — making it one of the best locations in the world for Northern Lights viewing from September to March.

Activities in Abisko

  • Northern Lights viewing (September to March)
  • Midnight Sun hiking (June to July)
  • The Aurora Sky Station cable car
  • King's Trail (Kungsleden) hiking — Sweden's most famous long-distance trail
  • Dog sledding and snowshoeing in winter
  • Birdwatching and wildlife spotting
  • Fishing in the pristine rivers and lakes
